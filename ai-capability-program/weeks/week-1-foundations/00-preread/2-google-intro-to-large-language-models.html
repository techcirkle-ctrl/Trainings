How Large Language Models Work

Preread 2 · wrapper  •  ~1 hr  •  Google Cloud — Introduction to Large Language Models
Why this matters to you

Preread 1 told you generative AI predicts. This one shows you the engine doing the predicting — the LLM — and its two hard limits. Knowing those limits is what lets you tell a realistic AI plan from a fantasy one when your team brings you proposals.

By the end you'll be able to explain, in plain language, the two things an LLM cannot do on its own — and why that's exactly what the rest of Week 1 is about fixing.

The frame

An LLM is trained by reading enormous amounts of text and learning to predict the next piece — one token (a word-chunk) at a time. Do that at huge scale and it picks up grammar, facts, reasoning patterns, and tone. That's the engine under Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — all of them.

Two limits fall straight out of how it's built, and they drive everything that follows in this program:

The two limits that shape every design decision

LimitWhy it existsWhat fixes it (later this week)
It only knows its training data — blind to your business and to anything recentKnowledge is frozen at training timeRAG — feed it your documents at question time
It can only produce text — it can't take actionsIt outputs words, nothing elseAgents + tools — let it actually do things

Hold these two. They're the reason the "AI Evolution Stack" (preread 5) has the shape it does.

The other lever you control is the prompt. Because the model just continues from what you give it, the quality of your instruction — role, task, constraints, examples — largely determines the quality of the output. That's why prompt engineering gets a full hour in the session.

Carry these three away

  • Next-token prediction is the whole trick — repeated fast, at scale, it looks like understanding.
  • The context window is its working memory: only what you've put in this conversation. Close the chat and it forgets.
  • Model choice is a real decision — bigger/newer isn't always right; you trade off capability, speed, and cost (you'll meet the Claude family in the session).
