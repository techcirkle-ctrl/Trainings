AI Capability Program · Week 1 — Foundations

The AI Evolution Stack

Preread 5 · authored  •  ~1 hr  •  LLM → RAG → Agents → Multi-Agent
Why this matters to you

Every AI initiative you will ever sponsor sits on one of four layers. Knowing which layer a problem needs is the single most useful judgement an AI-capable leader has — it decides cost, risk, build time, and whether the thing will actually work.

By the end of this lesson you'll be able to look at a workflow in your own function and name the lowest layer that solves it. You'll bring that answer into Hour 1 of the session.

The one idea

Each layer adds one capability the layer below it lacks — and you only climb when the layer below genuinely can't do the job. Reaching too high is the most common and most expensive mistake.

Layer 1 · LLM

A reasoning engine

A large language model reads and writes language, reasons over what it's given, and draws on general knowledge learned in training. On its own it's brilliant but blind to your business and forgets every conversation.

In your function Draft a customer email, summarise a long report you paste in, brainstorm options.
it can't see your live data, and can't do anything beyond producing text.
Layer 2 · RAG

The engine, grounded in your documents

Retrieval-Augmented Generation fetches the relevant pieces of your own content at the moment of the question and hands them to the model, so answers are grounded in your reality and can cite their source.

In your function "Answer only from our latest pricing & policy docs." A grounded Q&A assistant over your team's knowledge.
it still only answers — it doesn't take actions or complete multi-step work.
Layer 3 · Agent

The engine, given tools and a goal

An agent is an LLM that can use tools, take steps in a loop, and keep state — look something up, decide, act, check the result, continue — until a goal is met.1 This is where AI stops answering and starts doing work.

In your function "Take an inbound RFP, pull the right specs, draft a quote, flag anything needing my sign-off." Multi-step, with actions.
one agent juggling many specialised jobs gets unreliable and hard to govern.
Layer 4 · Multi-Agent

A team of specialised agents, coordinated

Several focused agents (each good at one thing) coordinated by an orchestrator. Powerful for genuinely complex workflows — and the layer with the highest cost, latency, and governance burden. Most problems never need it.1

In your function A full deal-desk: one agent researches the account, one prices, one checks compliance, one drafts — orchestrated end-to-end.
complexity and cost — reach here only when a single agent provably can't cope.

The leader's decision lens

Start at the bottom. Climb only when the current layer can't do the job.

If the problem is…You need at most…
Generate / rewrite / summarise text I give itLLM
Answer questions from our own documents, with sourcesRAG
Complete a multi-step job that takes actions in toolsAgent
Coordinate several distinct specialised jobs at onceMulti-Agent

Rule of thumb: the cheapest layer that works, wins. Anthropic's own guidance is to find the simplest solution and only add complexity when it demonstrably improves outcomes.1

Check yourself

Pick the lowest layer that solves each scenario. Instant feedback — this is the point of the lesson, not a test.
