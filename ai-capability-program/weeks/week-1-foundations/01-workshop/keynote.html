AI Capability Program · Week 1

It's Monday morning. You already know AI is changing everything.

You've read the articles. You sat through the briefing. Then you open your laptop — and the work in front of you is exactly the same as it was last year.

The gap nobody names

You are AI-aware. You are not yet AI-capable.

Knowing the wave is coming is not the same as knowing how to ride it. Today, that flips. By Friday you stop being a spectator.

The engine

Right now, AI is a black box you're told to just… trust.

It sounds confident. It's sometimes confidently wrong. And you can't tell which — so you either over-trust it or you don't use it at all.

The engine, understood

It doesn't look things up. It predicts.

Once you feel that in your gut, the magic and the failure mode both make sense — and you become the one who decides when to trust it.

The stack

Treat AI as one big lump of magic, and you overbuild — or freeze.

Every problem looks like it needs the most powerful, most expensive thing. That's the costliest mistake in the room.

The stack, climbed

It's a ladder. The cheapest rung that works wins.

Prompt → grounded knowledge → agent. Place any problem on the right rung and you've made a leadership call worth millions, on purpose.

From asking to building

Today you ask a chatbot questions, one at a time.

It answers from thin air, it forgets your business, and nothing actually gets done while you sleep.

From asking to building

Grounded in your documents. Acting on your behalf.

An answer with sources you can defend. Then a step beyond: something that takes the goal and does the work — an agent, not a chat.

By Friday

You'll hold a spec
for the one problem
worth solving.

One real task from your own function — mapped, scoped, and chosen. The single thread you carry through all four weeks.

In four weeks

It's not a slide.
It's deployed —
and your team uses it Monday.

A working agent in your real workflow, plus a 90-day plan you sponsor. Not a certificate. Capability.

So let's begin
